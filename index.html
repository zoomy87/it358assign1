Differences between Mobile and Traditional development

        The first difference between these two intrinsicly linked areas of development is the context and user interaction and user experience. Here developers and stakeholders make the mistake of trying to mimic the same capabilities that one could get from a desktop. That is a mistake because when a person uses a mobile device their interactions are much quicker, and the mobile device has less power to process the data. So if you port your desktop site to a mobile environment many users won't even be there when the site actually loads. This links to understanding how important the users' exerperience is. If your app is too slow, confusing, or ugly they will move to another becuase in this day in age there is always another app to use.

        The next things to understand are the limitations of a touch based interface and the best practices that are associated with each platform. On a mobile device real estate is very limited and button size is important not simply a design based on taste. If the button is too small then your user can't click it, and if it's too big then you lose out on the very limited space. One also has to take into consideration button placement because if it is out of reach of the users thumb it makes your app more difficult to use. When following best practices it is ok to put a twist on the app to make it unique, but do so knowing that if it is too different it can be confusing to your user. If you make things look like buttons that aren't buttons it can be confusing. So try to stick with the conventions of the platform you are developing for.

        The final three things to consider are perceived speed, release cycles, and security of your applications. If your app isn't fast then on mobile you will lose the users, which means you have to have a fast app or do you? Turns out you don't have to have a fast app simply one that seems fast. What I mean by this if your app is slow then you can add touch states to the buttons or unique loading animations. Keeping to the agile development philosophies will go far for mobile development because one has to have a short release cycle for updates to your app. Many companies are pushing out product and improving their apps and that means you have to as well. Security is had thing to do because nothing is 100% secure and then we have users that for the most part don't have a first clue about mobile security practices. So when creating your app keep in mind that you have to balance security with accessibility so as you don't annoy or turn off the user.
